1. Family

Family is considered the primary "school of life," where a teenager's identity, emotional security, and communication skills are first developed.

●​ Low (0–12): At this level, the home environment is often characterized by significant instability, frequent conflict, or crisis. The individual may feel completely alone, believing that no one in the family understands them. Communication is typically difficult, leading the young person to navigate pain, absence, or dysfunction in silence. Caregivers might be entirely absent or largely disengaged from the child's life. Sharing feelings or dreams at home is often met with conflict or dismissal, forcing the individual to keep things to themselves. Furthermore, negative habits or patterns within the family dominate, making it a struggle for the individual to resist harmful influences.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): Your score of 14/25 indicates you have a foundational support system but face visible gaps. While you might feel mostly supported, there are occasional misunderstandings that hinder total openness. The home is generally stable but experiences minor tensions or unpredictable moments. You may have one active caregiver, but another might be less engaged. Sharing feelings happens occasionally in safe moments, rather than being a constant habit. Your family habits are likely a mix of good and harmful patterns. Growth requires identifying a trusted adult to confide in and focusing on intentional communication to bridge these gaps.​ ●​ High (21–25): A high score represents an environment of full support where open communication and love are standard. The home is a peaceful, consistent, and very stable place for all members. Both parents or caregivers are actively involved, supportive, and present. The home serves as a safe space where feelings, struggles, and dreams can be shared completely and freely. Strong, godly values are consistently modeled by role models in the house. These families prioritize healthy habits that positively shape the individual’s life and future.

2. Finance

This domain examines financial wisdom, moving from inherited patterns of anxiety or debt toward independence and biblical stewardship.

●​ Low (0–12): Your score of 11/25 highlights this as a priority area requiring urgent attention. A low score often means the individual has no clear idea of how finances work or how to manage them. They may live in an environment of serious debt or poverty that affects every aspect of daily life. Saving is non-existent, and money is typically spent as soon as it is received. Borrowing is often viewed as a normal or fine practice without a plan for repayment. Additionally, there is a mindset of scarcity where the individual feels they must keep everything for themselves, preventing them from being generous.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): Individuals in this range understand financial basics but lack a consistent, disciplined plan. Financial stress may occur frequently, impacting their overall wellbeing even if they are not in a total crisis. They might save money occasionally when they have extra, but it is not yet a fixed habit. They understand that borrowing is risky and try to avoid unnecessary debt, but they may still struggle with complete financial discipline. Giving to others or God happens only when they feel they can afford it, rather than as a core commitment.​ ●​ High (21–25): High scorers actively manage their money with a clear plan based on biblical values. They come from families that are financially disciplined and generous. Saving is a non-negotiable daily or weekly habit for them, regardless of the amount. They are fully aware of the dangers of unplanned debt and may even teach others how to avoid it. Giving is a core lifestyle value; they consistently tithe and look for ways to serve others with their resources. These individuals have successfully broken harmful cycles of financial mismanagement.

3. Government

This area explores one's relationship with systems like school or the nation and the responsibility to lead with integrity.

●​ Low (0–12): A low score suggests a complete lack of awareness regarding the laws and systems that govern the community. Corruption or injustice in the environment may deeply harm the individual or their family. There is a tendency to avoid responsibility, often blaming others when things go wrong. The person likely feels powerless against large systems, believing they are too small to make a difference. Consequently, they may ignore injustice or even join in on wrong behaviors simply to survive.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): Your score of 13/25 reflects developing civic awareness and a growing sense of responsibility. You are likely aware of basic rules and generally follow them. You notice the effects of corruption but have learned to manage within the system. While you try to be accountable, you may still struggle with full responsibility at times. You are starting to see yourself as someone who can influence the environment in small ways. Growth involves finding mentors who model integrity and practicing courage even when no one is watching.​ ●​ High (21–25): High scorers are very aware of their rights and responsibilities as citizens and are actively engaged in their communities. They live in relatively just environments or have learned to maintain their integrity despite environmental challenges. They own their actions fully and encourage others to be accountable as well. These individuals actively seek out ways to be a positive influence and lead in their communities. They consistently stand for justice and truth with courage, even if doing so carries a personal cost.

4. Spirituality

Spirituality is the foundation of character and identity, focused on one’s relationship with God and practicing spiritual disciplines.

●​ Low (0–12): This level indicates an individual who may not believe in God or rarely thinks about spiritual matters. There are no spiritual practices such as prayer or reading Scripture. A large gap exists between any stated beliefs and their actual daily actions. They have likely never considered the idea of a spiritual gift or calling. They are not connected to any faith community, such as a church or prayer group, and live without the guidance of spiritual accountability.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): Your score of 16/25 suggests you have a faith foundation, but it is not yet the primary driver of your daily life. You believe in God, but your relationship may be inconsistent or focused on times of crisis. Spiritual habits like prayer occur occasionally rather than daily. You are working on aligning your actions with your values, but inconsistencies remain. You may have some ideas about your spiritual calling but need further guidance to develop them. Achieving a high level requires committing to daily disciplines and finding an accountability partner.​ ●​ High (21–25): For high scorers, a personal relationship with God is the absolute foundation of their existence. Daily spiritual disciplines, including prayer and Scripture reading, are non-negotiable habits. Their lives consistently reflect the "fruit of the Spirit," showing high alignment between belief and action. They have a clear identification of their spiritual gifts and are actively growing in them. They are deeply rooted in a faith community where they serve, lead, and remain accountable to others.

5. Talent

Talent is viewed as a God-given calling that must be cultivated through daily discipline and intentional mentorship.

●​ Low (0–12): At this level, the individual has no idea what their core talents or strengths are. They never invest time in developing any specific skills and may feel they have nothing to offer. Often, they are living out a dream placed on them by others rather than pursuing their own passions. They work entirely alone without any guidance or coaching. They view talent only as a tool for personal success rather than a gift to be used for the benefit of others or to honor God.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): This range describes someone who has some guesses about their talent but lacks total clarity. They invest time in their gift occasionally, but it is not yet a consistent habit. Their path is often a mix of their own dreams and the expectations of those around them. While they might receive informal advice, they lack a structured development plan. They are beginning to see how their talent could serve others, but they have not fully integrated this into their life goals.​ ●​ High (21–25): Your score of 18/25 places you in this high-achieving category. You have full clarity on your God-given gifts and actively develop them with daily discipline. You are pursuing a dream that is clearly defined and uniquely yours. You have intentional mentorship and a clear plan for your future development. Most importantly, you view your talent as a responsibility to be stewarded for the glory of God and the service of others. You are encouraged to protect this momentum by ensuring your talent remains pointed toward service.

6. Training

Knowledge is seen as the bridge to one's calling, requiring excellence in both formal and informal learning environments.

●​ Low (0–12): Your score of 12/25 sits at the edge of this priority range. A low score often means the person is completely lost regarding their educational path, simply following what their peers do. They are disengaged from their current studies and put in very little effort. Educational choices are usually made under external pressure rather than personal passion. There is no understanding of how training relates to long-term purpose. These individuals rarely focus on skills outside of basic schooling, leading to a lack of preparation for their future.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): Those in the moderate range have some direction but may not be fully committed to their path. Their effort in studies varies, and while they attend classes, they may not always pursue excellence. They are starting to make connections between their talent and their training, though their educational choices are still influenced by external advice. They occasionally pick up extra skills like communication or leadership. To grow, they must have honest conversations about whether their current track truly serves their God-given calling.​ ●​ High (21–25): High scorers are fully aligned with an educational path tailored to their specific gifts and calling. They are fully committed to pursuing excellence in all their studies. Their educational decisions are intentional, made with prayer and wise counsel. They have a fully integrated understanding of how training, talent, and purpose work together. Beyond school, they actively follow a development plan to gain leadership and trade skills. They see knowledge as a tool to reach the destination God has set for them.

7. Service

Service is defined as the mark of true greatness and is essential for breaking selfishness and building empathy.

●​ Low (0–12): Your score of 10/25 indicates this is a primary focus area for growth. A low score means the individual rarely thinks about serving others and may only do so when forced. They tend to keep all their time and resources for themselves. Helping others might feel like a burden, or they may even resent being asked to help. Their career plans are focused purely on personal success with no thought for others. There is zero involvement in volunteering, peer support, or community contribution.​ ●​ Moderate (13–20): At this level, service happens occasionally or when it is convenient. The person may give some resources to help others if they feel they have enough to spare. In the right situation, they feel a sense of fulfillment from helping, but it is not yet a regular source of joy. They are starting to consider how their future career might serve others, but it is not a core part of their vision. They might participate in minimal or one-off volunteer events without deep engagement.​ ●​ High (21–25): High scorers have made service a consistent and non-negotiable part of their lives. Generosity is a lifestyle for them, and they regularly budget their time and resources to help those in need. They find deep meaning and joy in serving others. Their entire professional vision is designed to serve and give back to the world. These individuals often lead or organize service initiatives in their community. They follow the example of leadership through service, focusing on the needs of others above their own.​
